Man's Greatest Fear

Posted on October 11, 2025 by Covenant

Being human is a gift.

Some never agree to this tho.

"Why am I alive? Why did I get born into this world? Against my own volition."

Since time immemorial, the curiosity of man yet fails to be satisfied.

Science invented as a means to know more, to see more, to learn more.

Always striving to be at the peak of knowledge, reveals a fear nested deep within:

"I have to be the strongest"

The Paradox of Knowledge

But what is strength without understanding? What is knowledge without wisdom?

Man fears irrelevance. To be forgotten. To be surpassed. To be... ordinary.

So we chase:

  • Achievement after achievement
  • Discovery after discovery
  • Innovation after innovation

Yet the void remains.

The Real Enemy

Perhaps man's greatest fear isn't death, failure, or even obscurity.

It's meaninglessness.

The realization that despite all our efforts, all our science, all our progress—

We might just be specks screaming into the void, hoping someone hears.

But here's the thing...

Maybe that's okay.

Maybe the gift of being human isn't about being remembered or being the strongest.

Maybe it's simply about being.

About experiencing this absurd, beautiful, terrifying existence.

Even if just for a moment.

So yeah, man fears a lot of things.

But the greatest fear? That none of it matters.

And maybe the greatest courage? Living fully anyway.

The Bug at 3AM

Posted on September 28, 2025 by Covenant

There's something poetic about debugging at ungodly hours.

The world sleeps. Your IDE glows. The cursor blinks like it's mocking you.

"It was working yesterday..."

Classic. But was it tho?

The Journey

Hour 1: This should be easy. Just a small logic error.

Hour 2: Maybe I need to refactor this entire module.

Hour 3: Questioning every life decision that led to this moment.

Then... you find it. A missing semicolon. A typo in a variable name. A logic gate you inverted.

And somehow, the universe makes sense again.

What I learned...

Bugs don't care about your sleep schedule.

Code doesn't care about your feelings.

But the satisfaction of finally running that green test? Chef's kiss.

That's what keeps us going. That dopamine hit of solving the puzzle.

Why I Farm (No, Really)

Posted on August 15, 2025 by Covenant

People find it weird when I say I'm into farming.

"You're a dev. Why plants?"

Here's the thing: farming is just another system to optimize.

The Parallels

  • Plant wrong season? Null pointer exception in real life.
  • Overwater? Memory leak. Your plant drowns in resources.
  • Ignore pests? Technical debt that compounds exponentially.
  • Harvest too early? Premature optimization. Ruins everything.

But unlike code, you can't just git reset --hard and start over.

Plants teach you patience. They grow on their timeline, not yours.

"Nature doesn't negotiate with your sprint deadlines."

And when you finally harvest what you planted months ago?

That feeling hits different from deploying to prod. It's... tangible.

You can literally eat the fruits of your labor. Try doing that with a React component.
